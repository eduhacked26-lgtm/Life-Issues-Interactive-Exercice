FIVE A DAY

[1] Do you eat well? Do you take regular exercise? Do you get enough sleep? These are some of the life concerns that health experts are worried about. Diet has become a central life issue in many countries, including Tunisia, where fast food culture is gradually replacing the traditional balanced diet. Nutritionists and dietitians warn that a diet deficient in fruits and vegetables is one of the biggest health risks people face today.

[2] Health authorities in many countries promote the idea of eating five portions of fruit and vegetables every day — widely known as "Five a Day." The idea is simple: a healthy, balanced diet protects us against serious illnesses such as heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and some cancers. Yet studies show that a large number of people — particularly young people — fail to follow this advice. Their lifestyle often involves little exercise, too much screen time, and poor eating habits.

[3] The impact of poor diet is not only physical. A lifeless routine — waking up late, eating processed food, and spending hours on social media — can also affect mental health. Young people who adopt a healthier lifestyle report feeling more energetic and positive about life. The connection between what we eat and how we feel is, according to experts, a lifelong lesson everyone must learn. Fortunately, making a change does not have to be dramatic. Small steps can make a surprising difference to your quality of life.
